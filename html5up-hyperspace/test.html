Network Engineer

Network engineer’s participant in the design the network infrastructures based on client needs/requirements, assessing information flow throughout and beyond a network system and ultimately the final implementation of the network. Network infrastructures include that of servers, switches, routers, firewalls, authorised devices, and several other electronic components. Performing tasks such as creating and reinforcing a network's firewalls provide the network with adequate protection to prevent data loss or compromise. Additional responsibilities include the successful integration of different parts such as the setting up and configuration of additional devices/equipment.

Network engineers operate closely to their network architecture counterparts, this is as network architects design the network diagrams whilst the engineers create and implement these changes. Additionally, they also communicate closely to general staff to remain up to data with the status of the network, and to be aware in the event of an error. The communication between each department is crucial to the health of the network system, as the network professionals should be notified as soon as possible as soon as an error occurs to ensure minimal damage.

Problem solving complications within a network when different software and hardware have compatibility issues during implementation into a network. Daily tasks can also include regular diagnosis and troubleshoots for the network systems to ensure that everything operates properly, and network correction when errors occur within the system. This can be through performing network upgrades, reinforcing firewalls, managing security configurations and checking for potential errors within the system.

Problem solving issues within the network, determining compatibility issues, and working around them to ultimately implement the item into the system network. Network engineers may be required to take lead within internal investigations within a network to identify faults and errors, which requires foresight to ensure the investigations minimise downtimes to prevent any major disruptions to the companies operations.

This allows businesses to focus more on achieving business objectives as there is no longer a constraint in terms of accessing data, as anyone with authority can access their network servers given, they have sufficient connection.
Performance: The best and most expansive Cloud computing services run on networks that contain data centres which consistently are upgraded to the latest and fastest technologies to allow fast and efficient cloud computing services. These upgrades allow for little network latency and increased performance.

Autonomous Vehicles

What does it do?

While currently cars are driven by people, autonomous vehicles are vehicles controlled without humans. This is done with cameras and other sensors on the outside of the car analysing the surrounding situation, so that the car can move under computer control rather than human operation. Ultimately, autonomous driving technology aims to reduce accidents, traffic jams and provide people with safe and comfortable driving.

Currently, there are still no perfect Autonomous vehicles that can drive on public roads. However, development using 5G (high-speed and large-capacity data transmission) is underway as state-of-the-art technology. Information detected by the vehicle sensor is transmitted to the cloud to collate large quantities of data related to congestion status and accident status. Autonomous vehicles are built with mechanisms to predict danger by acquiring the necessary information from the stored data.

For now, it is possible to enable autonomous driving under certain circumstances. TOYOTA recently launched a vehicle equipped with autonomous driving technology that can be used on highways with speeds less than 30km/h. Tesla, which is famous for its autonomous driving technology, has already sold vehicles that can self-park, change lanes, and autonomously drive on highways under certain conditions. However, their Society of Automotive Engineers level is 2.

-Definition of Society of Automotive Engineers-
Level 0. No driving automation: Driver performs all dynamic driving tasks.
Level 1. Driver Assistance: This system can control the movement of either the vehicle acceleration or deceleration, or handle operation. The vehicle can be kept safe at a vehicle-to-vehicle distance between the front and rear of the car.
Level 2. Partial Driving Automation: The system can control acceleration, deceleration and steering at the same time. It has the function to help the vehicle stay in the lane or self-parking features.
Level 3. Conditional Driving Automation: The environment detection function allows the system to perform all driving tasks. However, If the failure occurs the system requires operation by a human.
Level 4. High Driving Automation: The system can replace all driving operations. Even if the system fails, it can be operated automatically without human intervention. However, humans have the option of driving manually.
Level 5. Full Driving Automation: The difference between level 4 is the height of the quality of the automatic operation technology. It does not require human intervention and provides a higher and more sophisticated service than human driving.

There is a big gap between level 2 and 3 automation, but many vehicle manufacturers are eagerly developing it. When it comes to level 3 vehicles, the ‘driver’ is not responsible for any accidents that occur (at least in Japan).

If development goes well, vehicles equipped with Level 3 advanced autonomous driving technology will definitely be sold within three years for use on public roads. Ultimately, each country must amend the law to prepare for autonomous vehicles running on public roads to ensure that any issues are under responsibility of the appropriate parties. Since the autonomous vehicle of level 3 is going to spread, it is expected to alleviate the congestion on the highway by vehicles that perform the appropriate operation in accordance with the situation. In addition, it will lead to suppressing the fatigue of drivers.

In addition, in order for the automatic driving level to improve to 3 and 4 in the future, it is necessary to improve the judgment performance of AI. The system must be able to safely protect pedestrians and drivers by having AI judge all the measures to be taken in the unlikely event that the vehicle is unable to drive due to bad weather during driving or just before an accident occurs. Therefore, it is necessary to improve the performance of AI.

It is predicted that the way we use automobiles will change as autonomous driving becomes practical. After we no longer need human driving, we can spend our free time in the car doing business, talking with friends or family, sleeping, etc. rather than focusing on driving cars ourselves.

Until now, many packages have been transported to various places by trucks operated by drivers, but the elimination of drivers reduces transportation costs and leads to lower product prices. There is also the possibility that new services will be born. It allows vehicles to become small shops and do business anywhere on the road. This also leads to cheaper sales of products because there is no need for land charges and sales staff at the store. Another advantage of this service is that you can sell it in areas where it was previously difficult to purchase. For example, areas with a high elderly population, or low-population areas. However, it is predicted that some jobs of driving a car will disappear with the spread of autonomous vehicles, such as bus drivers and taxi drivers. Automation has eliminated the need for jobs such as typists, telephone operators, and elevator operators so far. Likewise, the profession of driver is likely to disappear. In addition to this, if store-free services using autonomous driving become widespread, retail sales staff and shopping centres may become unnecessary. Furthermore, as an effect of autonomous driving, families will be "using a car without owning it”. This will cause the number of people who don't have a driver's license to increase, as it will no longer be needed, and autonomous taxi costs are cheaper so the number of people who use taxis will grow larger. Therefore, automobile manufacturers will not sell vehicles to individual customers, but instead sell to companies that use vehicles in the future.

Internet of Things (I.O.T) Sensors

What does it do?

The rapid progression of technology in the world has changed our way of life in many ways unimaginable to anyone in the 20th century, making what used to be tedious tasks into a simple one. As society is progressing towards automation, inventors have designed and implemented many new technical devices to aid in the field of automation including the Internet of Things.

The Internet of Things defines the vast, diverse range of physical items which have been integrated with sensors, software and other technologies which allows data exchange over a network and/or the internet. The implementation of such technologies allows objects to connect to the internet to collect, compile and transport data without the aid of human input, allowing for actions to take place autonomously.

A section of IoT includes sensors, which are devices which are able detect and measure change, respond to external information and communication detections and actions across the internet. These different sensors come in different forms, detecting, and responding to different external environments. Sensors include temperature sensors, which identifies the heat energy levels of a certain source, pressure sensors that detect changes in pressure, proximity sensors that will sense an item/objects distance between the device, accelerometers, which track the rate of change in an object's speed and optical sensors which converts rays of light into electrical signals.

Currently there are around 7 billion different types of IoT devices, by the time of 2025 it is estimated there will be 25 billion. As the technology advances and time passes, IoT devices have been and will continue to integrate themselves into all kinds of different areas of expertise. Some examples of possible future projects could be; a smart agriculture system, one which could monitor the produce, and automatically do the entire farming process, from sowing the seeds to the harvesting of the finished product, possibly even notifying the farmers if there are variables which would make it unsuitable to grow the produce. These actions could be received as feedback and sent to a phone device, giving all sorts of useful data to the farmer. Another project idea could be a weather reporting system. With the use of temperature, humidity, and rain sensors, it could relay the data and possibly even predict weather for the coming days, given the changes in the temperature, wind direction and humidity of the air. Lastly, the simplest yet most likely project that is to arise from IoT technology, an fully automated home system. Having full control over all appliances via an application on a smart device would be one of, if not the most useful thing that could come about from implementing IoT technology into their household to a general person. Being able to simply change the temperature from an air conditioner, or turn off the stove or lights, all from simply pressing a button on a phone would make the everyday life of a general person so easy and convenient.

This development is possible due to the advancement of normal technology, and the basic human drive to always make technology smarter, more powerful and for it to be convenient and efficient. IoT devices are the simple evolution of technology, once the technology has reached its peak in its function, all that is left is to improve the device by having it do its designed task automatically. As doing so will allow even more progression, reducing the workload on humans and giving them even more time to try to improve the technology.

What is the likely impact?

The biggest improvement that development of the internet of things will bring, is that it will help with local and global interconnectivity. While our current world is very interconnected, the ability to have all devices linked into one big network allows people from different parts of the world to much more easily communicate with each other.

Another big change that would be brought around is that many monotonous tasks would be able to be don’t without human intervention. Tasks such as transferring data and files could be set up, collating relevant information from across the world. File storage would likely also be improved, as the interconnectivity could facilitate files forming backups with less effort.

The final big improvement that IoT will bring about is ease of access. Currently, many devices are majorly different methods of use. With IoT, people could access the functions of devices that they don’t understand from devices they have used and are comfortable with.

This change would likely help people who have very little experience with computers, such as elderly people, as it would allow them to have the convenience of all devices, while only needing to understand a single device.

Similar to all new technologies, the biggest negative impact is that it has the capacity to make certain jobs redundant. As IoT links many devices and systems together, human input can be removed from many processes. Some jobs which could be replaced by this technology are things like airspace coordinators, where they take aeroplane locations and weather conditions into account, when deciding if more aeroplanes can take off or land. This same job could be done by computers, which could be further helped by further connections between all devices. In the past, similar things have happened, with jobs like tellers being replaced by ATMs, as machines are better equipped to deal with the information given.

How will this affect you?

Personally, as a young adult, the internet of things is mostly a beneficial thing, as it helps predominantly with making the basic tasks of everyday life even easier. The fundamental principles of advancing technology into IoT technology are to allow a seamless ease of control over all aspects of our technology in our household, from our basic home appliances to the security system of a home.

Depending on the advancement of technology, one of the key differences could be allowance of complete power over monitoring and manipulating every aspect of a house. Be it simply turning off the lights or changing the temperature of an air conditioner, all whilst simply touching a button on a mobile device. This could even allow someone to be instantly notified, if say a sensor is triggered once an unexpected person arrives at the front of their house, possibly even activating the home’s protection shutters. These actions could possibly happen even if you were away from your home, as long as the system at home is still active and is connected to your mobile device.

When it comes to possible changes affecting family members, it is dependent on the age of the family member. Some of the more elderly members would most likely see the technology as foreign and cumbersome, possibly ignoring its existence and forgo even using it. While younger family members would most likely quickly adapt to the change in technology, as it would just be something else which they just started to learn. This also applies to affecting my friends, as most of my friend groups at this current age are, like myself, young adults. They should have a smooth transition, quickly adapting to the change and implementing the technology into their lives as well.
